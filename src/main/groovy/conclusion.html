Conclusion

Based off all the information given, pollution almost seems impossible to stop. Factories need to dispose of their waste and humans are exponentially populating and don't even care enough to pick up their trash. Countless fish die everyday, factories increase the chance of global warming, and thousands of lakes become inhabitable and there is no way to stop it. WRONG.

In a sense, pollution is a lot like not voting. When voting season comes around, many people get out to vote; however, there is always that one person that you know that says, "I'm just one vote. It doesn't matter if I vote or not." This is similar to a polluter. The polluter says, "I can throw this candy wrapper out. It's just one wrapper." In both cases, they are absolutely correct; neither of those single instances matter. However, if the nonvoter was in the United States in 2008, there were another 180 million people that didn't vote with him. The problem wasn't the single instance, it was the group collaboration of instances. The same concept applies to the polluter. It's not one instance that led to the pollution we have now; it's the fact that instances happen often enough for pollution to have a large effect on society. Like I said in the introduction: given time, our human pollution will eventually be destroyed by the environment; however, we are overwhelming the environment with too much trash to handle at one time. That's why pollution has gotten so bad.

So now the question is: "How can our nation solve the problem of pollution, or at least cut pollution down to a size the environment can handle?" There are many ways to come about this solution. One way is to leave everything in the hands of the government. The government implements "command and control" policies, where they try to control environmental pollution by regulating activities that hurt the environment. Examples of command and control acts are the Clean Air Act of 1970 and 1990, which is when the government tried to regulate the amount of emission from automobiles. The government can keep making acts like these, but in no way is the government getting their money back from it, making it very costly. However, in my opinion, the government shouldn't have to spend a lot of money on this - if any. The people should be more responsible. All it takes is a small amount of courtesy to wait until the next trash can to throw away your trash. If companies were to spend money on research, they can find a cost effective way of disposing their waste. The only kind of pollution that cannot be stopped is accidental pollution (BP oil spill), but other than that, pollution can be avoided.
